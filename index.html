About

Two vocations, one discipline.

At 51, Rev. Iliesa Tagilo carries two very different formations into one calling. He trained first as a mechanical engineer at the University of the South Pacific, learning to think in systems, tolerances, and structures — and later answered a call to ministry, completing a Master of Divinity in preparation for pastoral and theological leadership.

For over twelve years he has served the Methodist Lay Training College in Davuilevu, Nausori, now as Vice Principal alongside Rev. Rusiate Tuidrakulu, the college's Principal. That engineer's instinct for building things that hold together shows up in his ministry work too: in institutional systems he has strengthened, and in curricula he has designed from the ground up.

"He builds what the college stands on — its structure, its standing, and what it teaches."
